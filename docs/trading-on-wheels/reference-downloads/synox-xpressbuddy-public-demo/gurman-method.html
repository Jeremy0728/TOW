The Gurman Method

  • Home
  • Gurman Method

A structured options methodology built around rules, discipline and recurring income education.

One strategy. Clear rules. Less noise.

A method for studying recurring income strategies without depending on market predictions.

The Gurman Method is Trading On Wheels' proprietary educational framework for applying the Wheel Strategy with structure and discipline. It helps investors understand market context, select quality assets, sell options with a plan, manage assignment and review trades through a repeatable process.

The goal is not to predict every market move. The goal is to follow a method that makes decision-making clearer, calmer and more consistent.

  • Based on the Wheel Strategy.
  • Focused on option premiums as an educational framework.
  • Built around asset selection, risk management and process.
  • Designed for investors who want structure, not speculation.

What the Gurman Method is and what it is not

Clear expectations protect the learner and the process.

It is a rules-based process

A structured way to apply the Wheel Strategy with asset selection, review and risk awareness.

It is a learning framework

A method for building confidence through repetition, review and community education.

It is not a guarantee

No course, membership or strategy can guarantee profit, performance or financial freedom.

It is not blind signals

The objective is to understand the decision-making process, not copy ideas without learning.

How the method works

The process is designed to reduce uncertainty by giving each decision a clear role.

  • Understand market context

    Review the general market environment before selecting opportunities.

  • Select quality assets

    Focus on stocks or ETFs that meet the method's criteria because assignment must be part of the plan.

  • Sell cash-secured puts

    Use put options to collect premium while defining the price where ownership may be acceptable.

  • Manage assignment with a plan

    If assigned, the position is handled as part of the Wheel process, not as a failure.

  • Sell covered calls

    Once shares are owned, covered calls can be used to continue the Wheel process.

  • Review, adjust and repeat

    Every trade should strengthen review habits, risk control and consistency.

Principles of the method

The method is built to keep decisions practical and disciplined.

Rules over predictions

Decisions are based on process, not emotion or headlines.

Risk first

Every trade begins with the question: what can go wrong and how will it be managed?

Quality assets only

The Wheel Strategy requires discipline because assignment is always possible.

Recurring income mindset

The focus is repeatable premium education, not chasing the next big trade.

Simplicity

Fewer strategies, fewer distractions and a clearer learning path.

Continuous learning

Each trade becomes a case study for better future decisions.
