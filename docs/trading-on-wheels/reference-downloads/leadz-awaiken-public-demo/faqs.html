frequently asked questions

Trading On Wheels

What is Trading On Wheels?

Trading On Wheels is Oscar Gurman's education and community platform built around the Gurman Method, the Wheel Strategy, and disciplined trade review.

What is the Gurman Method?

It is a repeatable process for reading market context, selecting trades, defining risk, executing with rules, and reviewing the decision.

Is this financial advice?

No. TOW is education and practice. Traders are responsible for their own decisions, risk, and account management.

Who is Oscar Gurman?

Oscar is the founder of Trading On Wheels and the educator behind the Gurman Method.

Why does TOW focus on process?

Because consistency comes from repeatable decisions, not from guessing the next move.

Where should I start?

Start with the course for foundations, or join the membership if you want ongoing market context and trade logic reviews.

Membership

What is included in the membership?

The membership gives access to the private room, market context, trade logic, weekly reviews, trade cards, and community accountability.

Is the membership only for advanced traders?

No. It is best for traders who want structure and are willing to follow rules, review decisions, and learn consistently.

Does the room provide trade alerts?

The room focuses on trade logic and education. Ideas may be reviewed, but the goal is to understand the reasoning behind them.

How many founding members are allowed?

The current founding membership is planned around limited access so the room can stay focused and accountable.

frequently asked questions

Course

What does the course teach?

The course teaches options foundations, the Wheel Strategy, trade selection, risk management, and the full Gurman Method.

Can beginners take the course?

Yes. The course starts with foundations and builds toward structured trade review and execution.

How is the course different from the membership?

The course teaches the system in order. The membership shows how that system is applied around current market conditions.

Competitions and risk

Are competitions traded with real money?

No. Competitions are designed for rule-based practice so traders can test discipline and execution with clear structure.

How are competition rankings calculated?

Rankings are based on performance, rule compliance, risk control, consistency, and execution quality.

Why does TOW use competitions?

Competitions let traders practice under pressure while focusing on process, rules, and review.

Does TOW guarantee returns?

No. TOW does not guarantee returns. The focus is education, process, risk awareness, and disciplined practice.
